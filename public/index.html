Compliance &
Documentation Systems

ISO 9001-backed documentation infrastructure for semiconductor fabs — SOPs, audit readiness, EHS reporting, and traceability systems that keep projects compliant at every stage.

0 of 0 complete
ASOP Development & Document ControlFoundation
Guidance
SOPs are the backbone of fab compliance. Every procedure must be version-controlled, reviewed, and traceable. Fabs expect documented evidence that SOPs exist, are current, and have been acknowledged by the relevant staff. Start with the highest-risk processes first (EHS, cleanroom access, contractor onboarding) and build outward.
! WARN
ISO 9001 requirement: All documents must have a unique identifier, revision date, approval signature, and defined review cycle. Undated or unsigned SOPs will fail a supplier audit immediately.
Create SOP master template with required fields
Template must include: Document ID, Title, Version, Effective Date, Review Date, Author, Approver signature, Scope, Purpose, Procedure steps, Related documents. Use consistent header/footer across all SOPs.
High
Build document control log (master register)
Spreadsheet or system tracking every SOP: Document number, Title, Current version, Owner, Last reviewed, Next review date, Status (Active/Obsolete/Draft). This IS the audit trail.
High
Draft priority SOPs: cleanroom access, contractor onboarding, EHS incident reporting
These three are asked for first in every fab supplier audit. Cleanroom SOP must cover gowning sequence, prohibited items, and violation reporting. Contractor SOP must cover vetting, badging, orientation acknowledgment, and site-specific rules.
High
Establish review and approval workflow
Define who drafts, who reviews (technical accuracy), who approves (management authority), and who distributes. Document this process itself as an SOP. All approvals must be signed and dated.
Med
Set up obsolete document controls
Old versions must be marked OBSOLETE and removed from active use but retained for minimum 3 years. Never delete — auditors may request prior versions to demonstrate change history.
Med
BAudit Readiness & ISO 9001 AlignmentCritical
Guidance
Precision holds ISO 9001 certification — this must be demonstrated, not just stated. Prepare a supplier qualification packet so you can respond to RFQ requests within 48 hours.
i INFO
Micron and GlobalFoundries both conduct supplier audits. They will request your ISO 9001 certificate number, issuing body, scope, and most recent surveillance audit date. Have these printed and in a folder at all times.
Prepare supplier qualification packet
Include: ISO 9001 certificate (copy), certificate scope statement, QMS overview (1-page), SAM registration printout, GSA MAS contract summary, small business certifications (WOSB/EDWOSB/SDB/8a), UEI number, and contact sheet. Keep digital and physical copies.
High
Document internal audit schedule and results
ISO 9001 requires internal audits at planned intervals. Maintain an audit schedule, completed audit reports, and corrective action records. Auditors will ask: "When was your last internal audit? What findings? How were they closed?"
High
Create and maintain CAPA log
Every nonconformance must be logged, investigated (root cause), corrected, and verified. CAPA log is reviewed in every ISO surveillance audit.
High
Complete RBA Code of Conduct self-assessment
Required by Micron, Samsung, TSMC, and Qualcomm. Download the RBA self-assessment questionnaire from responsiblebusiness.org. Complete all sections covering labor, ethics, health & safety, environment, and management systems.
High
CVendor & Subcontractor DocumentationOngoing
Guidance
Fabs hold Precision responsible for the compliance of every subcontractor and vendor. Every vendor file must be complete before work begins — not after.
Build vendor pre-qualification packet template
Required fields: Business name, address, SAM registration, insurance certificates (GL, workers comp, auto), W-9, licenses, certifications, reference contacts, signed code of conduct acknowledgment. Every vendor gets a file before first PO.
High
Create subcontractor compliance verification system
Track insurance expiry dates, license renewals, and certification expirations. Set calendar alerts 60 days before any document expires. A lapsed insurance certificate can halt work on a fab site with no notice.

Capability 02 · Workforce Ops

Workforce
Infrastructure

End-to-end workforce pods deployable in 30–60 days — contractor onboarding, cleanroom orientation, LMS tracking, administrative staffing, and compliance reporting dashboards.

0 of 0 complete
AContractor Onboarding SystemDay 1 Priority
Guidance
Semiconductor fabs have zero tolerance for unvetted individuals on site. Every contractor must complete a defined onboarding sequence — no exceptions. Build the system so that no badge is issued until all steps are confirmed complete.
! WARN
Critical: Cleanroom access requires documented acknowledgment of gowning procedures, contamination protocols, and prohibited items. A contractor who enters without this documentation creates liability for Precision.
Design contractor onboarding packet (master template)
Include: personal info form, emergency contact, identity verification checklist, signed NDA, site rules acknowledgment, cleanroom protocol acknowledgment (if applicable), drug test consent, background check consent, photo ID copy, and badge application. Each page must be signed and dated.
High
Create cleanroom gowning orientation guide
Step-by-step gowning sequence: booties, coverall, hood, gloves, face mask order and technique. Include prohibited items, contamination reporting procedures, and cleanroom conduct rules. Must be site-specific for each fab client.
High
Establish badge management and access tracking log
Track every badge: contractor name, issue date, access level, expiry date, areas permitted, and return status. Badges must be deactivated the day a contractor's engagement ends. Maintain log for 3 years minimum.
High
Build onboarding status tracker (per engagement)
Track each contractor through stages: Applied → Background check → Drug test → Orientation → Documentation complete → Badge issued → Active. Color-code by status. At any point a fab client should be able to see exactly where each contractor stands.
Med
BLMS / Training Records SystemCompliance
Guidance
Every training event must be documented — who attended, what was covered, when, who delivered it, and the worker's acknowledgment signature. Verbal training does not exist in an audit. If it isn't written down with a signature, it didn't happen.
Set up training record system (LMS or structured spreadsheet)
For each training event capture: Employee/contractor name, Training title, Date, Duration, Instructor/provider, Pass/fail or completion status, Expiry date (if recertification required), Signed acknowledgment on file. System must be searchable by person and by training topic.
High
Create training matrix (role × required training)
Map every role against all required training modules. Matrix shows at a glance who needs what. Gaps in the matrix = compliance gaps. Update whenever new training requirements are added.
High
Build compliance reporting dashboard
Weekly/monthly report showing: total headcount, onboarding completion rate, training completion by module, certifications expiring in next 30/60/90 days, open corrective actions, badge status summary. Send to fab clients to demonstrate operational control.
Med
Establish expiry alert system for certifications
OSHA 10/30, First Aid/CPR, cleanroom certifications all have expiry dates. Set alerts at 90, 60, and 30 days before expiry. Expired certifications must be renewed before the worker continues in that role.
Med
CWorkforce Scaling & Pod DeploymentGrowth
Guidance
The 30–60 day deployment promise requires pre-built systems, not ad-hoc setup. Every tool, template, and workflow should be ready to replicate before an engagement starts. Think of it as a franchise kit.
Build workforce pod deployment playbook
Document every step from contract signed to pod operational: Day 1–5 (client kickoff, site rules review, template customization), Week 2–3 (system configuration, staff orientation, initial onboarding runs), Week 4–8 (full operations, first reporting cycle, client review). Assign task owners and deadlines.
High
Develop administrative workflow optimization guide
For high-volume onboarding (100+ contractors), document batch processing workflows: how to schedule orientation sessions in groups, how to run parallel document collection, how to manage exceptions without slowing the main queue.

Capability 03 · EHS & Risk

Safety & Risk
Compliance

Complete workforce safety infrastructure — background screening, drug testing program management, OSHA coordination, and ISO 45001-aligned safety documentation for fab environments.

0 of 0 complete
ABackground Screening & Workforce VettingPre-Access
Guidance
Background screening is a condition of access on semiconductor sites. The screening program must be consistent, documented, and defensible. Every screening decision must be logged with the criteria used.
! WARN
FCRA compliance required: Background checks must comply with the Fair Credit Reporting Act. Use a compliant third-party consumer reporting agency. Obtain written consent before screening. Follow adverse action procedures — failure to do so creates legal liability for Precision.
Select and contract FCRA-compliant background screening vendor
Vendor must provide: criminal history (federal + county), sex offender registry, SSN trace, employment verification, education verification. Confirm they provide FCRA-compliant adverse action notices and consumer dispute resolution.
High
Document screening criteria and adjudication matrix
Define clearly: what results automatically disqualify, what results require case-by-case review, and who has authority to make final access decisions. Apply criteria consistently — inconsistent application creates discrimination liability.
High
Create adverse action procedure and documentation
FCRA requires a two-step process: (1) Pre-adverse action notice with copy of report and Summary of Rights; (2) Reasonable waiting period (typically 5 business days); (3) Final adverse action notice if decision stands. File all notices for 7 years.
High
Build screening status tracking log
Track every candidate: name, date consent received, date submitted, date results received, adjudication outcome, decision date, decision maker, adverse action sent (if applicable).
Med
BDrug Testing Program ManagementRequired
Guidance
Drug testing programs must be written, published, and consistently applied. The program must define testing types, substances tested, collection procedures, MRO process, and consequences of positive results.
Draft written drug and alcohol testing policy
Policy must cover: program scope, testing occasions (pre-employment, random, post-incident, reasonable suspicion, return-to-duty), substances tested (DOT 5-panel minimum), collection method, MRO review process, consequences of positive/refusal, employee rights, and confidentiality protections.
High
Contract certified collection site and MRO
Collection must occur at a SAMHSA-certified lab using DOT chain-of-custody procedures. MRO must be a licensed physician. Get written agreements specifying turnaround time, result reporting method, and MRO availability for split sample requests.
High
Build drug test results and compliance tracking log
Maintain confidential log of all tests. Store actual test results separately in individual confidential files. Access restricted to authorized personnel only.
Med
COSHA Safety Training & Incident ManagementOngoing
Guidance
OSHA compliance is about documented proof that you took required precautions. Maintain the OSHA 300 log even if you have zero incidents — an empty log is valid evidence; a missing log is a violation.
i INFO
ISO 45001 alignment: GlobalFoundries, Micron, and Intel require ISO 45001. Document your OSHA program in ISO 45001 structure: hazard identification, risk assessment, operational controls, performance monitoring, and management review.
Verify OSHA 10/30 certification status for all site personnel
Confirm and document OSHA 10 for all workers, OSHA 30 for all supervisors. Cards must be from OSHA-authorized providers. Some fab sites require training within the past 5 years — confirm client requirements. Store copies of all cards in individual files.
High
Establish OSHA 300 log and maintain annually
OSHA 300 log is required for employers with 10+ employees. OSHA 300A summary must be posted February 1 – April 30 each year. Retain logs for 5 years. Even with zero incidents, maintain the log.
High
Create incident reporting and investigation procedure
For any injury, near-miss, or hazardous condition: initial report within 24 hours, investigation within 72 hours, root cause analysis documented, corrective actions assigned with due dates and owner, close-out verification. Near-misses treated with same rigor as actual incidents.
High
Build safety inspection checklist for site visits
Weekly/bi-weekly safety walk covering: PPE compliance, housekeeping, emergency exit access, fire extinguisher inspection dates, first aid kit inventory, hazardous material storage, signage, and equipment condition. Signed by inspector, dated, filed.

Electronics &

In-house NYIT-credentialed electronics engineer providing facility-side diagnostics, sensor calibration, PCB analysis, and sub-fab instrumentation support without OEM certification delays.

0 of 0 complete
ASensor Calibration ProgramANSI/ESD Priority
Guidance
Fab environmental tolerances are tight — deviations of 1–2% can cause yield failures worth millions. Every instrument used to control these parameters must be calibrated on a documented schedule with a traceable standard.
! WARN
ANSI/ESD S20.20 (Micron): Suppliers handling electronics must demonstrate ESD control — documented ESD training for all handlers, calibrated wrist strap testers, and a written ESD control plan. Non-compliant suppliers can be removed without warning.
Build instrument calibration register
Every instrument in the register: unique instrument ID, description, location, calibration standard used, calibration interval, last calibration date, next due date, calibration result, and calibration provider. Instruments past due must be flagged and taken out of service.
High
Establish calibration procedure for each instrument type
Document calibration method for: thermometers/temperature sensors (NIST-traceable reference), humidity sensors (salt solution reference points), pressure gauges, airflow meters. Each procedure must specify acceptable tolerance ranges and out-of-tolerance actions.
High
Create out-of-tolerance response procedure
When calibration finds an instrument out of tolerance: immediately take out of service, investigate if in-service measurements may have been affected, notify client if affected measurements were used in process decisions, repair/replace, re-calibrate, return to service with documentation. Log in CAPA system.
High
Draft ESD control plan (ANSI/ESD S20.20 aligned)
ESD plan must define: EPA (ESD Protected Area) boundaries, required PPE (wrist straps, ESD footwear, smocks), grounding requirements, packaging and handling procedures, testing frequency for ESD control equipment (wrist strap testers daily), training requirements, and audit/verification schedule.
Med
BPCB Diagnostics & Electronics BenchEE Capability
Guidance
The SCAN report identified PCB suppliers and lab support as active unmet needs in the NY fab ecosystem. The diagnostics bench must be documented so it can be presented credibly to procurement teams evaluating Precision for facility electronics work.
TIP
Positioning tip: Lead with what problems you solve — "We diagnose facility-side PCB failures without requiring OEM access or tool shutdown" — not just credentials. Buyers respond to problems solved.
Document electronics bench capabilities and equipment list
Inventory all diagnostic equipment: oscilloscope (model, bandwidth), multimeter, power supply, soldering/rework station, magnification equipment, signal generator, LCR meter. For each: model, calibration status, and capability range. This list becomes part of the capability statement.
High
Create PCB diagnostics work order and findings report template
Every diagnostics job must produce a written report: client name, date received, board description and part number, symptoms reported, visual inspection findings, test results, root cause determination, recommended repair or replacement, repair actions taken, and technician sign-off.
High
Document cleanroom-compatible work procedures
Electronics work in or adjacent to cleanroom environments requires contamination control: no outside tools without cleaning protocols, no soldering or chemical use without approved ventilation, components stored in ESD-safe cleanroom-compatible packaging.
Med
CEnvironmental Monitoring & Data LoggingFacility Support
Guidance
When Precision installs or supports an EMS, the system's data integrity becomes Precision's responsibility. Set-up documentation, data backup procedures, and alarm response protocols must all be documented before the system goes live.
Create EMS installation and configuration documentation template
For each system installed: sensor locations (with diagram), alarm setpoints and rationale, data logging interval, data backup schedule and location, network/integration configuration, access credentials management, and as-built documentation. Client receives a copy at project close-out.
High
Develop alarm response procedure for environmental excursions
Define response for each alarm type: who is notified (and in what sequence), what immediate actions are taken, when to escalate to client or fab engineering team, how the excursion is documented, and when production activities must be halted. Required for ISO 14001 alignment.
Med
Document cable harness and low-voltage work procedures
For cable fabrication: wire gauge standards per application, connector crimping procedures and inspection criteria, continuity test procedure (100% continuity and isolation testing required before delivery), labeling standards, and work order documentation. Test results must be recorded on the work order.
